Placement Overview

At Symbiosis Institute of Technology, Hyderabad, the Training and Placement (T&P) Cell serves as the cornerstone for enabling students to transition successfully from academia to industry. Guided by the values of excellence, innovation, and holistic development, the Cell strives to empower students to become future-ready professionals equipped with technical expertise, professional confidence, and industry-aligned competencies.

Our Mission

To empower students into becoming industry-ready professionals through a dynamic, inclusive, and future-forward talent transformation ecosystem, aligned with the ethos of excellence, innovation, and holistic development at Symbiosis Institute of Technology, Hyderabad.

Key Objectives

  • Organize campus recruitment drives with reputed companies
  • Prepare students through aptitude and coding assessments
  • Enhance personality development and communication skills
  • Provide career counselling with industry professionals
  • Build industry engagement through seminars and collaborations

Placement Goals

  • Nurture agile talent through structured training programs
  • Facilitate meaningful career pathways for students
  • Bridge the academia-industry gap with real-world exposure
  • Ensure 100% placement for eligible students
  • Increase Dream Offers (10+ LPA) by 30% annually

Training and Placement Cell Policy

Placement Policy Highlights

  • A student may apply to any number of IT/Core companies
  • After two job offers, students exit the placement process
  • Dream Offer (10+ LPA) eligibility: one per student
  • Minimum 60% academic score required throughout
  • All communication through T&P Cell

Eligibility Criteria

  • Minimum 60% across all educational stages
  • Regular attendance and discipline required
  • No active backlogs at time of placement
  • Positive recommendation from faculty mentor

Job Offer Policy

  • Maximum two regular offers per student
  • One Dream Offer allowed, doesn't count toward regular offers
  • In Day 1 scenarios, only one final offer can be accepted
  • Formal acceptance required within 48 hours

Internships

Structured internship opportunities designed to complement academic learning with real-world experience while maintaining institutional discipline.

Purpose & Scope

Purpose: Guide students in pursuing internships that complement academic and professional development.

Scope: Applies to all undergraduate students across all departments.

Objectives

  • Encourage industrial internships and research projects
  • Ensure alignment with academic goals
  • Provide attendance approval framework
  • Maintain institutional discipline

Eligibility

  • Completed second semester of study
  • Minimum CGPA of 6.0
  • No backlogs at time of application
  • Faculty mentor endorsement
